You Can Be on Streaming TV for the Price of a Facebook Campaign

Streaming TV used to mean a big production budget and a media buyer. Now you can run it for the price of a Facebook campaign.

For most of television's history, advertising on it was a fantasy for small businesses. A TV spot meant a big production budget, a media buyer, and a check with a lot of zeros — the kind of thing reserved for national brands and car dealerships with enormous ad budgets. If you ran a local service business or a small shop, TV simply wasn't a conversation you got to have.

That barrier is gone. The way people watch television changed, and in changing it quietly opened the door to exactly the businesses that were locked out before. Today, you can run ads on streaming TV for a budget in the same range as a modest social media campaign — and most small business owners have no idea it's even possible.

What changed: the TV audience moved

The shift is simple to describe. People stopped watching traditional cable and broadcast on the TV network's schedule, and started watching streaming services on their own schedule, on internet-connected TVs. This is Connected TV, or CTV — think ad-supported streaming on the big screen in the living room.

The audience followed the screens. Streaming now captures a larger share of total TV viewing than cable and broadcast combined, and the vast majority of U.S. households own at least one internet-connected TV device. The living-room audience didn't disappear. It moved to a place where, for the first time, small businesses can actually reach it affordably.

Why it used to be impossible — and now isn't

Old-fashioned TV advertising was expensive for structural reasons. You bought a broad time slot, paid for a big production, and hoped the right people were watching. There was no targeting and no small door to walk through — just one big, costly one.

Streaming TV advertising works completely differently, and the differences are exactly what make it small-business-friendly:

  • You buy by audience, not by broad time slot. Instead of paying to blanket everyone watching a given show, you can target the specific kind of customer you want — by location, interests, and more — so your budget isn't wasted on people who'll never buy from you.
  • You can start small. There's no enormous minimum. You can run a genuine streaming TV campaign on a budget that sits comfortably alongside your other digital channels.
  • Production is no longer a fortune. A polished streaming ad no longer requires a Hollywood crew. Modern tools have brought the cost of a good spot down to earth.

Put together, these changes turn TV from a channel priced for national brands into one a local business can actually use.

What streaming TV does that social can't

If the price is comparable to a social campaign, why bother? Because streaming TV does something social media struggles to: it puts your business on the biggest screen in someone's home, in full sight, without competing with a scrolling thumb.

A social ad shares the screen with a feed the viewer is actively swiping past. A streaming TV ad plays on the living room television, watched, with the viewer's attention on the screen. It carries the credibility and presence that TV has always had — the sense that a business "on TV" is real and established — but now at a price and with a precision that social-media budgets can reach. It's not a replacement for social. It's a powerful complement that makes your whole presence feel bigger.

The catch: buying it well matters

There's one honest caveat. Streaming TV is affordable and effective if it's bought and targeted well — and that's where a lot of small businesses could stumble if they went it alone. The targeting, the placement, the measurement of whether it's actually working: these take some know-how. Bought carelessly, any channel can waste money.

This is exactly where running it as part of a consolidated marketing operation pays off. When streaming TV sits alongside your search, social, and email in one coordinated plan, it's targeted at the same customers, measured against the same goals, and adjusted based on what the rest of your marketing is learning. You get the big-screen impact without the big-brand budget — and without the risk of buying it blind.

Television is finally within reach

The idea that TV advertising is only for big companies is out of date by about a decade. The audience moved to streaming, and the door opened for small businesses to reach the living room affordably, precisely, and without a fortune spent on production.
